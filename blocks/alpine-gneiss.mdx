Alpine Gneiss is inspired by the metamorphic rock common throughout the European Alps — characterised by its banded, foliated texture formed under intense heat and pressure. In Alpine Whispers it functions as a craftable stone type suited to rugged mountain architecture. All Alpine Gneiss blocks share the hardness and blast resistance of Cobbled Deepslate.

<Note>
All Alpine Gneiss blocks copy their properties directly from `Blocks.COBBLED_DEEPSLATE`. This means they match Cobbled Deepslate in mining speed requirement (needs a pickaxe), hardness, and blast resistance.
</Note>

## Frost-covered variants (Frosty blocks)

The Mossy and Mossy Layered brick groups implement the **Frosty block** mechanic. Each block tracks an internal `frosty` boolean state that overlays a snow/frost texture on top of the base mossy appearance.

**To add frost:** Right-click the block with a **Snowball**. Snowballs thrown as projectiles also trigger the effect. Snow particles burst from the block on contact.

**To remove frost:** Right-click the frosty block with a **Brush**.

Hold **[SHIFT]** while hovering over a frosty block in your inventory to read the tooltip reminder.

## Build ideas

Alpine Gneiss is well suited to builds that call for heavy, aged stonework:

**Castle walls and fortresses** — the large brick format and neutral grey-green tone read as imposing defensive masonry. Stack Gneiss Brick Walls with corner pillars of base Gneiss Bricks for a fortified silhouette.

**Mountain fortresses and watchtowers** — the layered variant's horizontal banding echoes the natural rock faces visible in alpine cliff sides, making it a strong choice for structures carved into or built against a mountain.

**Alpine chalets with stone foundations** — combine Arolla Pine wood upper storeys with an Alpine Gneiss ground floor for a traditional Alpine house profile. Mossy variants work well as partially buried cellar or basement walls, suggesting long exposure to damp ground.

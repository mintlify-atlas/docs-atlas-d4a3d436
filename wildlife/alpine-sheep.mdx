Alpine Sheep are the quirky highland mob of Alpine Whispers. They graze across frost-covered terrain, yield a distinctive decorative wool when sheared, and can be ridden across the slopes using a custom item. They also have an unusual — and risky — reaction to being fed bread, making them one of the more mechanically interesting mobs in the mod.

## Spawning

Alpine Sheep spawn in snowy biomes alongside other alpine wildlife. They graze naturally on **Hoarfrost Grass**, **Frozen Dirt**, and **Snow Blocks**, and can be found roaming open highland terrain.

## Shearing

Use **Shears** on an adult, unsheared Alpine Sheep to harvest **1–3 Homespun Wool**. The sheep must be alive, fully grown, and not already sheared for shearing to succeed.

After shearing, the sheep enters a sheared state (`Sheared = true`). Wool regrows when the sheep eats — once it grazes on a valid block, the sheared flag is cleared and it becomes ready to shear again.

## Riding

Right-click an adult Alpine Sheep (without sneaking) to mount it. However, the sheep will only respond to steering controls if the rider is **holding Bread on a Stick** in either hand. Without it, the sheep ignores movement input entirely.

**Boosting** is triggered by right-clicking while already riding and holding Bread on a Stick. This consumes one use of durability from the Bread on a Stick and gives the sheep a speed burst for **80 ticks** (~4 seconds) at **1.5× its base movement speed**.

| Boost parameter | Value |
|-----------------|-------|
| Boost duration | 80 ticks (~4 s) |
| Speed multiplier | 1.5× |
| Bread on a Stick durability | 25 uses |

## The bread effect

<Warning>
Feeding an Alpine Sheep a piece of Bread triggers a delayed negative effect. After approximately **10 seconds** (200 ticks), the sheep displays angry particles. After approximately **15 seconds** (300 ticks), it takes **2 damage** and the effect ends. Bread cannot be fed again for **80 ticks** after the effect is triggered, whether or not it is still active.
</Warning>

Feeding bread to an Alpine Sheep activates a timed sequence:

1. The sheep consumes the bread immediately with an eating sound.
2. After **200 ticks**, angry villager particles appear around the sheep.
3. After **300 ticks**, the sheep takes **2 points of damage**, the effect ends, and the internal timer resets.

This behaviour has a cooldown of **80 ticks** — you cannot trigger it again until the cooldown expires. The effect is cosmetic and mechanical, but does not kill the sheep outright under normal health.

## Grazing

Alpine Sheep use a custom `AlpineSheepEatBlockGoal` to graze periodically. The goal triggers at random intervals (roughly 1 in 1000 ticks for adults, 1 in 50 for babies). The sheep will eat from the following sources:

**Foliage (destroyed on eat, if mob griefing is enabled):**
- Hoarfrost Grass
- Tall Hoarfrost Grass
- Short Grass

**Terrain (grazes without destroying, except Grass Block → Dirt):**
- Grass Block
- Snow (layer)
- Snow Block
- Powder Snow
- Frozen Dirt

When the sheep successfully eats, `ate()` is called — this resets the sheared state, triggering wool regrowth.

## Wool & crafting

Shearing an Alpine Sheep yields **Homespun Wool**, a decorative block with a unique natural texture suited to alpine cottage builds. Homespun Wool can be crafted into **Homespun Carpet**, a flat variant for flooring and detailing.

Both blocks share the same aesthetic and work well as rustic alternatives to standard dyed wool in builds themed around highland or winter settings.

## Breeding

Alpine Sheep breed with **Wheat**. Feed two adults Wheat to put them into love mode. The resulting lamb grows into a full adult over time following standard Minecraft baby growth mechanics.

<Tip>
Keep a small pen of Alpine Sheep and breed them regularly for a steady supply of Homespun Wool and Homespun Carpet without needing to explore for new sheep.
</Tip>
